## Power

### P1 -- 2S battery input: J1(XH) -> F1 -> Q1(D->S) -> VM_BATT; J9 balance tap  [PASS]

Chain verified pin-by-pin: J1.1 -> F1.1 | F1.2 -> Q1.D | Q1.S -> VM_BATT.
2S pack window 6.0-8.4 V (firmware floor 6.6 V = 3.3 V/cell). Balance tap
J9 (XH-3): pin1 GND, pin2 cell-1 midpoint into R75/R76, pin3 pack+ shares
BATT_RAW (it IS the same terminal). No onboard charging -- J9 monitors only.
Battery on the DRAIN: correct reverse-protection orientation -- with a
reversed pack the body diode (anode=D) blocks; with correct polarity it
conducts until the gate enhances the channel.

*Nets exercised:* `BATT_RAW`, `BAT_MID`, `GND`, `Net-(Q1-D)`, `VM_BATT`

### P2 -- Q1 DMP3098L-7: Vgs rating, enhancement and conduction loss  [PASS]

R1 = 100k gate pulldown; Vgs = -VBAT = -6.0..-8.4 V.
Rating check: |Vgs| max 8.4 V = 42% of the
+/-20 V rating (netlist-declared; the 1S-era DMP2035U was +/-8 V
and 2S-UNSAFE -- this swap is the point of the part). -30 V / -3.8 A frame.
Enhancement: |Vgs| >= 6.0 V even at the pack hard floor, beyond the
-4.5 V Rds(on) spec point -> fully enhanced everywhere in the window.
Conduction loss at 60 mOhm: 0.9 A run -> 49 mW
(trivial for SOT-23); 3.6 A worst fault-transient -> 778 mW, bounded by the buck current limits + PTC (P3).
Gate leak through R1: 84 uA continuous (always-on; S4).

*Nets exercised:* `GND`, `Net-(Q1-G)`

### P3 -- PTC fuse MINISMDC260F/16 rating vs load  [PASS]

Netlist-declared PTC: 2.6 A hold / 5.0 A trip / 16 V
>= 8.4 V pack max (1.9x). Normal run ~0.9 A
battery-side, well under hold. Worst self-consistent draw at the 6.6 V
floor: 6V buck at its 3 A limit (3.04 A input) + 3V3 rail (0.57 A)
= 3.60 A -- inside the 2.6-5.0 A INDETERMINATE band, so the
PTC may or may not open under a double stall: it is NOT the stall
protection. Roles: PTC = hard faults only (a wiring short draws
VBAT/Rshort >> 5 A and trips fast); stall protection = the TPS54302's own
cycle-by-cycle limit + the firmware PWM constraint (M3) + TB6612 thermal
shutdown. Same role split as rev 5, re-derived for the 2S numbers.

### P4 -- U1 AP63203 3V3 buck: wiring, input-cap ratings, output caps  [PASS]

VIN 6.0-8.4 V inside the AP63203's 3.8-32 V range; fixed
3.3 V / 2 A output, ~1.1 MHz. Pin roles cross-checked against the netlist
pinfunction fields (IN/EN/SW/BST/FB), not just pin numbers.
Input bulk C1/C4/C18 10uF are netlist-declared 25 V parts (>= 2.9x the
pack max -- the 1S-era 6.3-10 V caps are banned on this rail); C2 100nF
HF bypass rides the same node (rating covered in the BOM).
Output caps C5+C7 = 44 uF ceramic (datasheet asks 2x22 uF class);
C10 10uF + distributed 100nF bypasses add margin.

*Nets exercised:* `GND`, `Net-(U1-BST)`, `PLUS3V3`, `PWR_EN`, `SW_3V3`, `VM_BATT`

### P5 -- 3V3 load budget vs the AP63203's 2 A rating  [PASS]

Peak 3V3 demand (everything at once): ESP32 500 + line IR 111 + wall IR 292 + indicators 20 + IMU 12 + misc 40 = 976 mA.
IR figures recomputed from netlist resistor values (120R line,
33R wall) at typical FET Rds -- the maximum-current corner.
AP63203 is a 2 A buck fed from 6.0-8.4 V (always stepping DOWN -- no
boost-region derating like the 1S TPS63001): headroom 51% over the everything-on worst case.

*Nets exercised:* `PLUS3V3`

### P6 -- L1 ripple current and saturation margin  [PASS]

### P7 -- U7 TPS54302 6V buck: FB divider sets 6.01 V, wiring, output caps  [PASS]

Netlist-read divider R73/R74 = 100k/11k with the

Output ceramics C16/C17 22uF/25V at the buck; the C30 bulk + TB6612 pin

*Nets exercised:* `FB_6V`, `GND`, `Net-(U7-BOOT)`, `SW_6V`, `VM_6V`, `VM_BATT`

### P8 -- L2 ripple, peak vs saturation, duty headroom at the pack floor  [PASS]

L2 = 4.7 uH at 400 kHz: ripple 909 mA pk-pk at
8.4 V in / 284 mA at 6.6 V (30% of
the 3 A rating -- coarse but standard for a 400 kHz SOT-23 buck).
Peak inductor current at the full 3 A ceiling: 3.45 A vs 3.5 A Isat
(1% margin -- thin, but the 3 A point is itself the
buck's limit, a transient state; normal running sits at 0.8-1.6 A. During
a hard overload the IC's peak limit + hiccup bounds the excursion.
Dropout: duty 91% needed at the 6.6 V firmware floor vs
~94% max (150 ns min off-time); below ~6.3 V input the rail
sags gracefully -- documented competition practice with Vbat feed-forward.

*Nets exercised:* `SW_6V`, `VM_6V`

### P9 -- Dual-switch logic: SW5 kills everything, SW6 kills motors  [PASS]

Truth table verified structurally: SW5 closed -> PWR_EN = 0 -> both EN
pins low -> everything off. SW6 closed -> MOT_EN = 0 -> motor rail off,
logic alive. R70 hangs off PWR_EN (netlist: R70.1), so MOT_EN can only
be high when the master switch already is -- both switches must be ON.
PWR_EN level with both open: R69 0M against the R70+R71 330k DC load
divides the pack (+ the AP63203's own 1.5 uA EN pull-up over 77k) to
6.56 V (8.4 V in) / 5.18 V (6.6 V) / 4.72 V (6.0 V hard floor)
-- NOT near-VIN as the R69-only analysis suggests. Still above the
AP63203's ~1.1 V EN threshold with >=1.6x margin, so the 3V3 rail does
start everywhere in the window; the knock-on effect on MOT_EN is P10's
subject. Off-state drain through this chain is metered in S4.

*Nets exercised:* `GND`, `MOT_EN`, `PWR_EN`, `VM_BATT`

### P10 -- MOT_EN level vs the TPS54302 enable threshold  [PASS]

Netlist-read chain: VM_BATT -[R69 0M]- PWR_EN -[R70 220k]- MOT_EN -[R71 110k]- GND.
With both switches open the three resistors form ONE string, so EN =
VBAT x R71/(R69+R70+R71), CREDITING both ICs' internal EN pull-ups
(TPS54302 ~1.2 uA over 82k, AP63203 ~1.5 uA over 77k x 0.33):
  8.4 V pack -> 2.29 V | 6.6 V pack -> 1.82 V   (threshold 1.21 V typ, 1.13 V min).
The R70/R71 values are correct for a STIFF source (2.80 V /
2.20 V would result, matching CONNECTIONS.md's '<=2.1 V'
note) -- the design forgot R69's 1M source impedance: R70+R71 = 440k loads
the 1M pull-up 2.3:1 and MOT_EN lands at ~a third of the intended level.
CONSEQUENCE: the TPS54302 never enables -- the motor rail is dead with
both switches ON. FIX (schematic, not this test): R69 -> 100k (PWR_EN
then sits at 81% of VBAT; MOT_EN = 2.28 V at 8.4 V /
1.79 V at 6.6 V, off-drain +76 uA) or re-ratio R70/R71.

*Nets exercised:* `MOT_EN`, `PWR_EN`


## Flashing

### E1 -- USB data path: J7 -> ESD array -> module DIRECT (22R pair deleted)  [PASS]

R59/R60 confirmed absent from the netlist; USB_DP/USB_DM are two-node
nets (U6 pass-through pins to the module's dedicated USB pads, IO19/20).
Rationale (standards review 2026-07-17): the S3's integrated FS PHY meets
the USB driver-impedance window internally and every Espressif S3 devkit
routes these pins directly -- series 22R was a legacy-PHY habit. The
USBLC6-2SC6 stays between the user-handled connector and the chip.
Flashing: plug in, hold A (BOOT strap IO0 low), tap RST -> ROM downloader
on the native USB-Serial-JTAG CDC. No external UART bridge anywhere.

*Nets exercised:* `USB_DM`, `USB_DM_C`, `USB_DP`, `USB_DP_C`

### E2 -- VBUS cable detect: divider into mux channel Y10  [PASS]

R67/R68 = 10k/15k -> 5.0 V reads 3.00 V, and the
Type-C worst case 5.5 V (vSafe5V upper bound) reads 3.30 V -- inside
the HC4067's input clamp (VCC+0.5 = 3.8 V) and the S3's 3.6 V IO
abs max when the channel is selected. Detection-only (threshold ~1 V of
swing), so riding the ADC's saturated top end at 5.5 V is acceptable.
VBUS does NOT power the board; rev 6 freed IO37 (now the IMU interrupt)
by moving this read onto the mux's Y10.

*Nets exercised:* `USB_VBUS`, `VBUS_SENSE`

### E3 -- USB-C CC pulldowns (UFP advertisement)  [PASS]

5.1k Rd on both CC pins: a C-to-C cable/charger recognises the board as
a UFP sink and enables VBUS -- without these, C-to-C flashing would fail.

*Nets exercised:* `Net-(J7-CC1)`, `Net-(J7-CC2)`

### E4 -- Boot straps rev 6: IO0 pulled up, IO45 pulled down, IO46 floats NC  [PASS]

IO0 (USER_BTN): 10k pull-up to 3V3, button A shorts to GND
-> released = SPI boot, held = ROM downloader.
IO45 (BIN2): 10k pulldown -> boots low -> VDD_SPI = 3.3 V
(correct for the WROOM); the TB6612's internal 200k input pulldown agrees.
NEVER add a pull-up to this net.
IO46 (BUZZ_CTRL): drives R81 220R -> Q34 (MMBT2222A) base -> buzzer. The
B-E junction only ever pulls the strap LOW = its required boot-msg default,
and the pin resets to input so the buzzer stays silent until app init.
Strap census: only IO0 is user-touchable, via the deliberate BOOT button.

*Nets exercised:* `BIN2`, `USER_BTN`

### E5 -- Reset circuit: EN RC + RST button  [PASS]

R11 10k + C9 1uF -> tau = 10 ms power-on reset
delay (Espressif asks >50 us after 3V3 valid; 10 ms is the classic safe
value). SW2 shorts EN to GND for manual reset; C9 also debounces it.

*Nets exercised:* `ESP_EN`, `GND`

### E6 -- UART0 boot-log contention guards on encoder pins  [PASS]

IO43/IO44 double as UART0 TX/RX during boot. The ROM prints its boot log
while the encoder outputs may drive the same lines: the 1k series guards
cap contention current at 3.3 V / 1k = 3.3 mA -- harmless to both the
encoder driver and the S3 pad. After boot the pins remap to PCNT inputs;
firmware keeps the console on USB-Serial-JTAG so UART0 never re-enables.

*Nets exercised:* `ENC2_A`, `ENC2_A_S3`, `ENC2_B`, `ENC2_B_S3`

### E7 -- JTAG header pin map and supply  [PASS]

1x6 header: 3V3, TMS(IO42), TCK(IO39), TDO(IO40), TDI(IO41), GND --
matches ESP-Prog's pin needs on the S3's dedicated JTAG quad. NOTE for
firmware: the S3 routes JTAG to USB-Serial-JTAG by default; to use this
header burn/strap the JTAG-sel (or use OpenOCD over USB, which needs no
header at all). Kept for crash debugging when USB itself is the casualty.

*Nets exercised:* `GND`, `JTAG_TCK`, `JTAG_TDI`, `JTAG_TDO`, `JTAG_TMS`, `PLUS3V3`


## Buttons

SW1 (A/BOOT): 'USER_BTN' -> GND when pressed.
SW3 (B): 'USER_BTN2' -> GND when pressed.
SW4 (C): 'USER_BTN3' -> GND when pressed.

## Motors

### M1 -- TB6612 IN/IN PWM mode: PWMA/PWMB/STBY tied high, IN pins from GPIOs  [PASS]

PWMA and PWMB verified tied to PLUS3V3 IN the netlist (not assumed), and
STBY pulled to 3V3 through R55 10k: the driver runs in IN/IN mode, PWM
applied to AIN1/2 + BIN1/2 (IO9/IO10/IO38/IO45). Modulating one IN with
the other low gives drive/coast; against the other high, drive/brake --
both firmware-selectable per the TB6612 truth table with PWM=H.
Safety: the TB6612's INx internal 200k pulldowns keep outputs off while
the MCU is in reset, and the hardware motor kill is now SW6 cutting the
entire 6V rail (P9) -- STBY's old GPIO/strap risk (IO46) is retired.

*Nets exercised:* `AIN1`, `AIN2`, `BIN1`, `BIN2`, `Net-(U2-STBY)`, `PLUS3V3`

### M2 -- VM pins on the regulated 6V rail; doubled outputs to connectors  [PASS]

All three VM pins land on VM_6V (6.01 V from P7's netlist math) --
inside the TB6612's 4.5-13.5 V operating window AND above the 4.5 V floor
below which the datasheet derates output current (the rev-5 1S design
lived in that derated region; rev 6's regulated 6 V restores the full
1.2 A avg / 3.2 A peak per-channel ratings, and the N20s get their rated
voltage at any state of charge). Every output uses BOTH package pins
(halves per-pin current) into the JST ZH direct-plug motor connectors.

*Nets exercised:* `MOTA_N`, `MOTA_P`, `MOTB_N`, `MOTB_P`, `VM_6V`

### M3 -- N20 stall vs TB6612 limits vs the 6V buck ceiling  [PASS]

N20 6 V motors: stall 1.6 A at 6.0 V (terminal R ~3.8 ohm).
(Envelope figure. The ORDERED motor -- robu GA12-N20 6V 200RPM, datasheet
2026-07-20 -- stalls at only 0.23 A / runs 60 mA rated: ~7x inside this
analysis. And the 8.4 V pack question is moot BY DESIGN: motors hang off
the REGULATED 6.00 V rail, never the raw pack -- at 8.4 V the buck still
delivers exactly 6 V to the motor terminals.)
Per TB6612 channel: 1.2 A continuous / 3.2 A peak. A single stall at 100%
duty (1.6 A) exceeds the continuous rating but sits at 50% of peak
-> legal as a PWM-limited transient; firmware caps duty so the AVERAGE
stays <= 1.2 A (duty <= 75% into a hard stall).
Supply ceiling: both motors stalled = 3.2 A demand vs the
TPS54302's ~3 A rating + cycle-by-cycle limit -- the buck (not the pack)
is the bottleneck and it limits/folds back rather than cooking anything;
C30 220uF + ceramics ride the 20 kHz PWM chop: worst-case droop
~76 mV per half-period at a 1.6 A edge -- the rail stays
stiff and the pack sees averaged, fused current (P3). TB6612 thermal
shutdown is the final backstop.

### M4 -- VM_6V decoupling chain at the driver + logic bypass  [PASS]

C30 220uF/16V alu bulk at the VM entry (2.7x rail
rating) + C11 10uF/25V + C12 100nF at the TB6612's VM pins absorb the PWM
edge current (motor hot-loop); C16/C17 22uF close the loop at the buck
(P7). C14 100nF bypasses the driver's separate VCC logic supply from 3V3.

*Nets exercised:* `GND`, `PLUS3V3`, `VM_6V`

## Wall sensors

### W1 -- IR333-A emitter chains and banked drive  [PASS]

Rev 6 emitters are IR333-A (5mm, 940nm, 20deg half-angle; netlist value
field names the part). Per-emitter with the netlist-read 33R:
(3.3 - 1.35) / (33 + 2ch*Rds) = 40 mA worst-case / 49 mA typical,

banks are normally pulsed (front/diag/side) for ambient rejection, and
the 20deg beam at ~50 mA gives championship-grade wall range.
Per-LED anode nets verified two-node against their own limiters: the
switched cathode node carries only the pair's summed current.

*Nets exercised:* `EMIT_DIAG_K`, `EMIT_FRONT_K`, `EMIT_SIDE_K`, `Net-(D1-A)`, `Net-(D2-A)`, `Net-(D3-A)`, `Net-(D4-A)`, `Net-(D5-A)`, `Net-(D6-A)`, `WALL_EMIT_DIAG`, `WALL_EMIT_FRONT`, `WALL_EMIT_SIDE`

### W2 -- PT334-6B receivers: bias, swing, direct ADC path  [PASS]

Each PT334-6B collector has a netlist-verified 47k pull-up; wall return
light (>=50 uW/cm2 at competition distances with the ~50 mA IR333-A
drive) swings the node 0.5-3.0 V directly into ADC1 pins IO1-IO6 (no
mux: all six walls sample in one burst). Black-lens part rejects visible
ambient; firmware subtracts the emitter-off baseline for the rest.
ASSEMBLY: PT334's LONG lead is the EMITTER (silk + docs note) -- opposite
of the LED convention.

*Nets exercised:* `WALL1_SENSE`, `WALL2_SENSE`, `WALL3_SENSE`, `WALL4_SENSE`, `WALL5_SENSE`, `WALL6_SENSE`

-> |Vgs| ~3 V >> |Vth| 0.8-2.0 -> LED lit at ~1.4 mA (1k, super-red

Full chains walked pin-by-pin: 3V3 -> Qsource/drain -> 1k -> LED -> GND.

### I2 -- Line indicator drivers (NMOS, LED ON = line seen)  [PASS]

BSS138 gate on LINEx_SENSE: black line -> PT dark -> sense pulled to 3V3
-> FET on -> LED lit. White floor -> sense saturates low (<0.4 V) < Vth
0.8 min -> off. Latched emitters (not pulsed) whenever indicators are

Full chains walked pin-by-pin: 3V3 -> 1k -> LED -> Qdrain/source -> GND.

## Mux

### X1 -- CD74HC4067: four GPIO selects (S3 promoted to IO8), enable, common  [PASS]

S0-S2 from IO11-13 walk I0..I7 = LINE1..8; rev 6 PROMOTED S3 from a GND
tie to IO8 (U3.12 -> U4.13, netlist-verified) opening channels I8-I15 --
battery/VBUS telemetry now rides I8-I10 instead of burning three ADC
pins. ~E stays hard-grounded (mux always enabled); one ADC pin (IO7)
reads all 11 sources. 100nF decoupling (C13) at the mux supply.

*Nets exercised:* `GND`, `MUX_S0`, `MUX_S1`, `MUX_S2`, `MUX_S3`, `MUX_SENSE`, `PLUS3V3`

### X2 -- Pack telemetry: VBAT divider into channel I8  [PASS]

R2/R3 = 100k/39k -> 8.4 V pack reads 2.36 V,
the 6.6 V firmware floor reads 1.85 V -- the whole window
lands inside the S3 ADC's calibrated 12 dB range with headroom.
Divider sits DOWNSTREAM of Q1 (on VM_BATT), drains 60 uA while
connected (metered in S4); C6 100nF makes the node stiff for the mux.
Firmware cutoff: 6.6 V pack = 3.3 V/cell.

*Nets exercised:* `GND`, `VBAT_SENSE`, `VM_BATT`

### X3 -- Per-cell telemetry: balance midpoint divider into channel I9  [PASS]

R75/R76 = 100k/100k -> a full cell 1 (4.2 V midpoint)
reads 2.10 V on I9. Firmware: cell1 = 2 x reading, cell2 = pack -
cell1 -- per-cell monitoring so EITHER weak cell triggers the 3.3 V/cell
cutoff. Divider drain 21 uA whenever the balance plug
is in (S4); C19 100nF stiffens the node for the mux hop.

*Nets exercised:* `BAT_MID`, `BAT_MID_SENSE`, `GND`

### X4 -- Mux Ron + source impedance vs ADC sampling  [PASS]

CD74HC4067 Ron ~70R typ / ~160R worst at 3.3 V -- negligible against the
sources: line channels up to 47k (PT dark), VBAT divider 28.1k,
midpoint 50k, VBUS 6k. 12-bit settling (8.3 tau into a ~10 pF sample cap):
worst 4.0 us (line dark) -> firmware budgets ADC sample-and-hold
time accordingly (or double-samples and keeps the second); the telemetry
channels' 100nF node caps (C6/C19) recharge through their dividers in
~3 ms RC -- fine at telemetry rates. Same firmware note as rev 5, now
covering 11 channels instead of 8.

*Nets exercised:* `MUX_SENSE`


## IMU

### U1 -- BNO055 supplies and protocol straps (I2C, addr 0x28)  [PASS]

Local bypasses at the IMU: C23 100nF + C24 10uF (the Bosch
reference pair for VDD/VDDIO on a shared rail).
PS1:PS0 = 00 (netlist-verified grounds) selects I2C; COM3/ADDR low ->
address 0x28; COM2 grounded as the I2C mode requires. VDD and VDDIO both
on the 3.3 V rail (single-supply, level-shift-free against the S3).
Supply current ~12.3 mA in normal fusion mode -- 1.3% of the P5 budget;
already counted there.

*Nets exercised:* `GND`, `PLUS3V3`

### U2 -- I2C bus: pin map, pull-ups, 400 kHz rise time  [PASS]

IO18/IO21 were freed by the TB6612 IN/IN rework (M1) -- the netlist ties
them to COM0/COM1 with 4.7k pull-ups. Rise time at a conservative
50 pF bus: 199 ns vs the 400 kHz fast-mode limit of 300 ns; the
pull-up value tolerates up to 75 pF. Sink current 0.70 mA << the
3 mA I2C VOL spec point -- comfortable for both parties. Control loop
reads at up to ~2.7 kHz theoretical; 100 Hz fusion output is the real cap.

*Nets exercised:* `IMU_SCL`, `IMU_SDA`, `PLUS3V3`

### U3 -- Interrupt line to IO37  [PASS]

BNO055 INT (push-pull, data-ready/any-motion) direct to IO37 -- the pin
freed when VBUS sensing moved onto the mux (E2). Point-to-point net, no
pull needed. The 500 Hz control loop may also simply poll; the interrupt
is for wake-on-motion at the start line.

*Nets exercised:* `IMU_INT`

### U4 -- Support pins: nBOOT/nRESET pulls, CAP bypass; internal oscillator  [PASS]

nBOOT_LOAD_PIN high through R79 10k = normal boot (low would enter the
Bosch bootloader); nRESET high through R80 10k -- no GPIO spent, the
watchdog/power-cycle covers recovery.
CAP pin: C20 100nF to GND (internal-LDO bypass, the
Adafruit reference value).
Clock: INTERNAL oscillator -- fusion time-base uses the on-chip clock
(crystal dropped for routability; internal osc is spec-supported).

*Nets exercised:* `Net-(U8-CAP)`, `Net-(U8-~{BOOT_LOAD_PIN})`, `Net-(U8-~{RESET})`

### S4 -- Always-on battery drain census (switches off, pack plugged)  [PASS]

With SW5 OFF both regulators are disabled (EN grounded / starved) but
four resistive paths stay on the pack (all values netlist-read, 8.4 V):
  R69 soft-switch pull-up 0M: 84.0 uA
  R2+R3 pack divider 139k: 60.4 uA
  R1 Q1 gate pulldown 100k: 84.0 uA
  R75+R76 midpoint divider 200k (via the balance plug): 21.0 uA
Total ~249 uA -- a 600 mAh 2S pack self-drains in ~0.3
years through the board; the LiPo's own self-discharge dominates. Rule
stays: UNPLUG BOTH CONNECTORS TO STORE. Note the midpoint path loads only
cell 1 -- another reason not to leave the balance lead in long-term.

*Nets exercised:* `BAT_MID`, `VM_BATT`
