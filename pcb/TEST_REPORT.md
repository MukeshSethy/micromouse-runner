## Power

### P1 -- Battery input path: J1 -> F1 -> Q1(D->S) -> VM_BATT  [PASS]

Chain verified pin-by-pin: J1.1 -> F1.1 | F1.2 -> Q1.D | Q1.S -> VM_BATT.
Battery on the DRAIN: correct reverse-protection orientation -- with a
reversed pack the body diode (anode=D) blocks; with correct polarity it
conducts until the gate enhances the channel.

*Nets exercised:* `GND`, `Net-(J1-Pin_1)`, `Net-(Q1-D)`, `VM_BATT`

### P2 -- Q1 enhancement and Vgs margin over battery range  [PASS]

R1 = 100k gate pulldown; Vgs = -VBAT = -3.0..-4.2 V.
DMP2035U Vgs(th) = -0.4..-1.0 V; |Vgs| at the weakest battery (3.0 V) is
3.0x the worst threshold -> fully enhanced (Rds 45 mOhm @ -2.5 V).
Gate leak through R1: 42 uA continuous (always-on).

*Nets exercised:* `GND`, `Net-(Q1-G)`

### P3 -- PTC fuse rating vs load  [PASS]

Worst self-consistent case at 4.2 V: 2 stalled motors (1.05 A
each) + WiFi burst (0.31 A battery-side) = 2.41 A.
0ZCJ0200FF2C: 2.0 A hold / 3.5 A guaranteed trip, 6 VDC >= 4.2 V. That
2.41 A sits in the 2.0-3.5 A INDETERMINATE band -- the PTC may or
may not open, so it is NOT the stall protection. Roles are: PTC = hard
faults only (a wiring short draws VBAT/Rshort >> 3.5 A and trips fast);
stall protection = the firmware PWM/current constraint documented in M2
plus the TB6612's own thermal shutdown. Normal running (<1 A) is far
below hold. (Audit-corrected: an earlier draft over-credited the PTC.)

### P4 -- TPS63001 input range, EN logic and soft power switch  [PASS]

VIN 3.0-4.2 V inside TPS63001's 1.8-5.5 V range.
Switch OPEN: EN = VIN - Ileak*R69 <= 0.1 uA * 1M = <0.1 V drop ->
EN >= 2.9 V >> VIH 1.2 V (run). Switch CLOSED: EN = 0 V < VIL 0.4 V
(shutdown, 0.1 uA typ). Off-state battery drain: R69 4.2 uA
+ VBAT divider 76 uA + Q1 gate 42 uA ~= 122 uA total (documented; unplug to store).

*Nets exercised:* `GND`, `PWR_EN`, `VM_BATT`

### P5 -- 3V3 load budget vs buck-boost capability  [PASS]

Peak 3V3 demand (everything at once): ESP32 406 + line IR 88 + wall IR 240 + indicators 21 + misc 20 = 775 mA.
TPS63001 capability at VIN 3.0 V (boost region; the buck-boost boundary
is VIN ~ 3.3 V): switch-limit math 1600 mA min * 3.0/3.3 * ~0.85 eff
~= 1.2 A, matching the datasheet typical-output curve -> ~40% headroom
over the everything-on worst case. (Audit-corrected figures.)

*Nets exercised:* `PLUS3V3`

### P6 -- VBAT sense divider scaling and ADC ceiling  [PASS]

R2/R3 = 22k/33k -> 4.2 V reads 2.52 V at IO8.
Source impedance 13.2k: fine for the S3 ADC with default
sampling; firmware should average 8+ samples.

*Nets exercised:* `GND`, `VBAT_SENSE`, `VM_BATT`


## Flashing

### E1 -- USB data path: J7 -> 22R series -> ESD -> module  [PASS]

Connector-side and module-side D+/D- nets are joined through 22 ohm /
22 ohm series elements with the USBLC6-2SC6 ESD array on the
connector side of the series resistors -- the ST-recommended topology for
USB 2.0 FS. The S3's native USB-Serial-JTAG needs no external driver:
plug in, hold A (BOOT strap IO0 low), tap RST -> ROM downloader on USB-CDC.

*Nets exercised:* `Net-(R59-Pad1)`, `Net-(R60-Pad1)`, `USB_DM`, `USB_DM_C`, `USB_DP`, `USB_DP_C`

### E2 -- VBUS cable detect divider  [PASS]

VBUS worst case is 5.5 V under Type-C source rules (vSafe5V upper
bound; an Rd-only sink cannot restrict it) -> 3.30 V at IO37
(10k/15k; ~3.33 V with 1% parts): below the 3.6 V
absolute max with slim-but-acceptable margin for a detect-only pin.
VBUS does NOT power the board. (Audit-corrected: 5.5 V, not 5.25 V.)

*Nets exercised:* `GND`, `USB_VBUS`, `VBUS_SENSE`

### E3 -- USB-C CC pulldowns (UFP advertisement)  [PASS]

5.1k Rd on both CC pins: a C-to-C cable/charger recognises the board as
a UFP sink and enables VBUS -- without these, C-to-C flashing would fail.

*Nets exercised:* `Net-(J7-CC1)`, `Net-(J7-CC2)`

### E4 -- Boot straps: IO0/IO45/IO46 power-on states  [PASS]

IO0 (USER_BTN): 10k pull-up to 3V3, button A shorts to GND
-> released = SPI boot, held = ROM downloader. 
BIN2/IO45 strap net 'BIN2': 10k pulldown -> boots low
STBY/IO46 strap net 'STBY': 10k pulldown -> boots low
IO45 low = VDD_SPI 3.3 V (correct for WROOM), IO46 low = valid boot-msg
strap; both idle-low nets (BIN2, STBY) so motor driver wakes disabled.

*Nets exercised:* `BIN2`, `STBY`, `USER_BTN`

### E5 -- Reset circuit: EN RC + RST button  [PASS]

R11 10k + C9 1uF -> tau = 10 ms power-on reset
delay (Espressif asks >50 us after 3V3 valid; 10 ms is the classic safe
value). SW2 shorts EN to GND for manual reset; C9 also debounces it.

*Nets exercised:* `ESP_EN`, `GND`

### E6 -- UART0 boot-log contention guards on encoder pins  [PASS]

IO43/IO44 double as UART0 TX/RX during boot. The ROM prints its boot log
while the encoder outputs may drive the same lines: the 1k series guards
cap contention current at 3.3 V / 1k = 3.3 mA -- harmless to both the
encoder driver and the S3 pad. After boot the pins remap to PCNT inputs.

*Nets exercised:* `ENC2_A`, `ENC2_B`

### E7 -- JTAG header pin map and supply  [PASS]

1x6 header: 3V3, TMS(IO42), TCK(IO39), TDO(IO40), TDI(IO41), GND --
matches ESP-Prog's pin needs. NOTE for firmware: S3 routes JTAG to the
USB-Serial-JTAG by default; to use this header burn/strap the JTAG-sel
(or use OpenOCD over USB, which needs no header at all). Header kept for
crash debugging when the USB stack itself is the casualty.

*Nets exercised:* `GND`, `JTAG_TCK`, `JTAG_TDI`, `JTAG_TDO`, `JTAG_TMS`, `PLUS3V3`


## Buttons

### B1 -- A/B/C/RST wiring and pull strategy  [PASS]

SW1 (A/BOOT): 'USER_BTN' -> GND when pressed.
SW3 (B): 'USER_BTN2' -> GND when pressed.
SW4 (C): 'USER_BTN3' -> GND when pressed.
A has the discrete 10k pull-up (R10, strap-grade). B (IO35) and C (IO36)
use the S3's internal ~45k pull-ups -- both pins are plain GPIOs on the
-N16 module (octal-PSRAM variants would consume them; module pinned in BOM).

*Nets exercised:* `USER_BTN`, `USER_BTN2`, `USER_BTN3`


## Motors

### M1 -- TB6612 control pin mapping vs truth table  [PASS]

All seven control nets land on their SSOP-24 pins (datasheet numbering)
and originate at module GPIOs. Truth table: IN1/IN2 = CW/CCW/brake, PWM
chops, STBY low = all outputs Hi-Z. STBY boots low (10k + internal 200k
pulldowns) -- motors cannot move until firmware asserts it.

*Nets exercised:* `AIN1`, `AIN2`, `BIN1`, `BIN2`, `PWMA`, `PWMB`, `STBY`

### M2 -- Motor outputs: doubled pins to connectors  [PASS]

Every output uses BOTH package pins (halves the per-pin current); the
routes are 0.3 mm (verified in the board file) -- IPC-2221 external 1 oz
gives ~1 A at ~10 C rise, adequate for transient PWM-limited stalls.
TB6612 per-channel limit: 1.0 A avg needs VM >= 4.5 V;
at VM 3.0-4.2 V the no-PWM DC limit is 0.4 A -- N20 stall (~1.05 A at
4.2 V) is legal only under PWM, which is the only drive mode the firmware
uses. Documented as a firmware constraint: never 100% duty into a stall.

*Nets exercised:* `MOTA_N`, `MOTA_P`, `MOTB_N`, `MOTB_P`

### M3 -- VM decoupling at the driver  [PASS]

C11 10uF + C12 100nF sit at the TB6612's VM pins (bottom face, under
the driver) absorbing PWM edge current; the bulk reservoir (C4 10uF +
C1 100uF) lives 15 mm away at the regulator input on the same VM rail.
C14 100nF decouples the driver's separate VCC logic supply from 3V3.

*Nets exercised:* `GND`, `VM_BATT`


## Encoders

### N1 -- Quadrature nets, pull-ups, PCNT capability  [PASS]

Each phase has a 10k pull-up to 3V3 (required if the encoder output is
open-drain, harmless if push-pull -- the N20 encoder boards vary) and
ENC2 additionally passes the 1k UART guards. Decoding: two S3 PCNT units
in hardware quadrature via the GPIO matrix -- zero-CPU-cost counting.

*Nets exercised:* `ENC1_A`, `ENC1_B`, `ENC2_A`, `ENC2_A_S3`, `ENC2_B`, `ENC2_B_S3`, `PLUS3V3`


## Line array

### O1 -- TCRT5000 LED drive current (shared bank FET)  [PASS]

Per-channel: (3.3 - 1.25) / (120 + 8ch*8 shared-FET ohms) = 11.1 mA
worst-case (Rds 8 ohm at Vgs 3.3); ~15 mA at typical Rds. Bank total
89-120 mA through Q19 (BSS138). TCRT5000 spec point is IF = 10 mA
(IC 0.5-2.1 mA), abs max 60 mA -> the drive brackets the spec point with
4x margin to the limit.

*Nets exercised:* `EMIT_LINE_K`, `LINE_EMIT`, `PLUS3V3`

### O2 -- Phototransistor load line and ADC swing  [PASS]

47k pull-up: white floor (IC ~0.8 mA) saturates the PT -> V <= 0.4 V
(VCEsat max); black line (IC < 30 uA) -> V >= 1.89 V.
Guaranteed swing >= 1.5 V (typically ~2.5 V) across the mux into IO7 --
ample for 8-way thresholding.
Sensing height: with 32 mm wheels the underside rides ~9.4 mm; the 7 mm
body puts the optical face at ~2.4 mm = TCRT5000's peak-response distance.

*Nets exercised:* `LINE1_SENSE`, `LINE2_SENSE`, `LINE3_SENSE`, `LINE4_SENSE`, `LINE5_SENSE`, `LINE6_SENSE`, `LINE7_SENSE`, `LINE8_SENSE`, `MUX_SENSE`


## Wall sensors

### W1 -- Emitter chains and banked drive  [PASS]

Per-emitter: (3.3 - 1.35) / (33 + 2ch*8) = 40 mA worst-case,
banks of two (80 mA per BSS138); ~54 mA at typical Rds. SFH 4550
abs max 100 mA continuous -> drive is 40-54% OF the limit (~50% margin);
the +-3 deg beam at ~40 mA gives championship-grade wall range. Banks are
pulsed by firmware (front/diag/side separately) for ambient rejection.

*Nets exercised:* `EMIT_DIAG_K`, `EMIT_FRONT_K`, `EMIT_SIDE_K`, `WALL_EMIT_DIAG`, `WALL_EMIT_FRONT`, `WALL_EMIT_SIDE`

### W2 -- PT334-6B receivers: bias, swing, ADC path  [PASS]

Each PT334-6B collector has a 47k pull-up; wall return light (>=50 uW/cm2
at competition distances with the 40 mA emitters) drives IC tens of uA to
mA -> 0.5-3.0 V swing directly into ADC1 pins IO1-IO6 (no mux: all six
walls sample in one burst). Black-lens part rejects visible ambient;
firmware subtracts the emitter-off baseline for the rest.
ASSEMBLY: PT334's LONG lead is the EMITTER (silk + docs note) -- opposite
of the LED convention.

*Nets exercised:* `WALL1_SENSE`, `WALL2_SENSE`, `WALL3_SENSE`, `WALL4_SENSE`, `WALL5_SENSE`, `WALL6_SENSE`


## Indicators

### I1 -- Wall indicator drivers (PMOS, LED ON = wall seen)  [PASS]

BSS84 source at 3V3, gate on WALLx_SENSE: wall seen -> PT pulls sense low
-> |Vgs| ~3 V >> |Vth| 0.8-2.0 -> LED lit at ~1.4 mA (1k, super-red
high-efficiency bin, clearly visible). No wall -> sense near 3V3 -> off.

*Nets exercised:* `Net-(Q28-D)`, `Net-(Q29-D)`, `Net-(Q30-D)`, `Net-(Q31-D)`, `Net-(Q32-D)`, `Net-(Q33-D)`

### I2 -- Line indicator drivers (NMOS, LED ON = line seen)  [PASS]

BSS138 gate on LINEx_SENSE: black line -> PT dark -> sense pulled to 3V3
-> FET on -> LED lit. White floor -> sense saturates low (<0.4 V) < Vth
0.8 min -> off. Latched emitters (not pulsed) whenever indicators are
enabled, so the LEDs read true floor state (rev-3 review finding).


## Mux

### X1 -- CD74HC4067 selects, enable, common and channel map  [PASS]

S0-S2 from IO11-13 walk Y0..Y7 = LINE1..8; S3 and ~E hard-grounded (only
8 channels in use, mux always enabled). Common Z drives IO7 through the
47k source impedances -- one ADC pin reads the whole line array.
100nF decoupling (C13) at the mux supply.

*Nets exercised:* `GND`, `MUX_S0`, `MUX_S1`, `MUX_S2`, `MUX_SENSE`, `PLUS3V3`


## System

### S1 -- Single unified ground; every subsystem returns to it  [PASS]

GND spans 86 pins on 71 components -- one net, no
split grounds (In1 is a solid plane, stitched at every SMD pour pad).

*Nets exercised:* `GND`

### S2 -- No floating module inputs / undocumented NC  [PASS]

Single-pin named nets: none (KiCad
explicit no-connects are excluded; they are flagged in the schematic and
documented in CONNECTIONS.md).

### S3 -- 3V3 rail fanout reaches every logic consumer  [PASS]

PLUS3V3 spans 62 pins: module, mux, both encoder
supplies, JTAG Vtarget, every pull-up and both indicator supply banks.

*Nets exercised:* `PLUS3V3`
